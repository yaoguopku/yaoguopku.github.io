Mining Comparative Reviews for Mobile App Comparison

- Introduction

As the number of mobile apps keeps increasing, users often need to compare many apps, in order to choose one that best fits their needs. Fortunately, as there are so many users sharing an app market, it is likely that some other users with the same preferences already made the comparisons and shared their opinions. For example, a user may state that an app is better in power consumption than another app in a review, then the review would help other users who care about battery life while choosing apps.

This project proposes a method to identify comparative reviews for mobile apps from an app market, which can be used to provide fine-grained app comparisons based on different topics. According to experiments on 5 million reviews from Google Play and manual assessments on 900 reviews, our method is able to identify opinions accurately and provide meaningful comparisons between apps, which could in turn help users find desired apps based on their preferences.

- Publication

  • Yuanchun Li, Baoxiong Jia, Yao Guo, and Xiangqun Chen. "Mining User Reviews for Mobile App Comparisons", 2017. (submitted)

- Experiment Data

  • Experiment process: We have done all the assessments with 9 different volunteers (who are not authors) who are good in English and familiar with mobile apps. Each piece of result is now labeled by 3 raters, and the majority opinion is taken as the ground truth in our evaluation results.
  • In practice, we have asked 6 raters to label whether a review is comparing two apps and 3 raters to label the topic and preference of the reviews. Each review is then labeled three times, and in over 95% of the cases, all three raters agree with each other. In the few cases when they do not agree, we take the majority opinion.
