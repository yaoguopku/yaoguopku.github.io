Projects for Potential Students

Here is a list of potential projects for incoming students who are interested in doing research with me. However, the list is neither exhaustive or exclusive, which means that potential students are welcome to propose any relevant new projects beyond this list.

- Operating Systems for the Future

    I am particular interested in building new types of operating systems (OSs) for new types of IT systems and beyond. A new OS may be built on top of the current OSs such as Linux and Windows, however offering similar functionalities such as resource management, software development kits (SDKs) and libraries, as well as application development and runtime support. Examples includes:

  • Robot OS. Operating systems for new robotic applications, such as robots and drones, especially for new programming models, programming supporting tools and environment for collective intelligence.
  • Brain-like OS. Operating systems for brain-like computing, including new programming models, programming support tools and compilers for new applications based on brain-like processor chips.
  • Internetware OS. Operating systems for Internetware, a new software paradigm for Internet computing. Internetware OS includes a new set of OS that can be applied to new types of applications and organizations. For example, we can build a new OS for a smart object, a university campus, a smart city or an enterprise.
  • Intelligent OS. Future OS will provide support for artificial intelligence (AI), while at the same time, AI will be built into all future OSs. We will investigate the application needs and design intelligent software development and runtime support.

- Quality Improvement of Mobile Systems

    We have been working on improving the quality of mobile applications and systems for edge devices such as smartphones and wearables.  Examples include performance measurement and improvement, mobile app analysis, power models, energy analysis and energy optimization of mobile apps and mobile analysis, privacy and security of mobile apps and sysems, etc. In particular, I am interested in the following projects:

  •  Understanding mobile app privacy. Help mobile users better understand why each mobile app uses sensitive permissions, where it is used and why it is used. As many mobile apps do not specify these detailed information, it is sometimes confusing to end-users why these permissions are used. We have been trying different approaches to help users understand the privacy implications of mobile apps. New approaches and ideas are needed as this is one of the under-studied issues in mobile security and privacy.
  • Fine-grained privacy policies and access control. One of our main goals is to invent better access control methods for mobile users to better control their personal data. 
  • Power estimation based on static analysis: Power and energy consumption is one of the everlasting issues for mobile devices. Although many techniques have been proposed in this directions, it is still difficult for a developer to understand how his/her app consumes energy. We aim to invent a new method to help developers analyze the power/energy consumption of their apps during the development phase.
