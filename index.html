Yao Guo is a full professor in the Institute of Software of the School of Electronics Engineering and Computer Science at Peking University. He has served as Vice Chair of Computer Science since 2013. His general research interests include operating systems, mobile computing and applications, low-power design and software engineering.

He received his PhD in Computer Engineering from University of Massachusetts at Amherst in 2007. His PhD advisor was Professor Csaba Andras Moritz. Previously, he got his BS and MS degrees, both in Computer Science, from Peking University in 1997 and 2000 respectively.

He is a member of ACM and IEEE, and a senior member of CCF.

Research Projects (Full publication list)

  • Analyzing the Mobile App Ecosystem: Static and dynamic analysis of Android apps, including clone detection [WuKong, ISSTA 2015], third-party library detection [LibRadar, ICSE 2016], dynamic testing of Android apps [DroidBot, ICSE 2017], purpose analysis of app permissions [UbiComp 2015, TOIS 2017], UI analysis for Android apps [UbiComp 2016, Honorable Mention Award], privacy-aware app development [PrivacyStreams, UbiComp 2017a]. Analysis and comparison on different Android app markets [WWW 2017, MSR 2018, ongoing], user review analysis [UbiComp 2017b].
  • New Types of OSs and OS Optimization: Compiler optimizations of the Linux kernel [APSys 2014, APSys 2015, MobileCloud 2018], performance benchmarking of Android smartphones [HotMobile 2017], similarity-based optimization of web browsers [WWW 2014]. New types of OSs, including ubiquitous OS [Computer 2018], Internetware OS [Science and Tech Review 2016], CampusOS [Internetware 2013], etc.
  • Low-Power/Green Computing: Energy modeling, optimization of embedded and mobile systems, including an online energy inspection tool for Android apps [ISLPED 2017], standby energy analysis and potential optimizations [MobileCloud 2016], power estimation based on batter traces [ISLPED 2013], as well as automatically fixing sensor-related energy bugs [ISLPED 2015].
